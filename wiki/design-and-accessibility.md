# Design & Accessibility Guidelines

These rules keep the live site readable, responsive, and accessible.

## Contrast
- Use black, white, and slate values that remain readable in both themes.
- Body text should stay at or above WCAG AA contrast on all supported backgrounds.
- Keep borders and focus rings clearly visible.

## Typography
- Use clear hierarchy for headings, labels, and supporting text.
- Avoid dense blocks of copy unless the content genuinely needs it.
- Keep monospace styling for identifiers, metadata, and machine-like labels.

## Interaction
- Every interactive control needs a visible focus state.
- Use semantic elements for buttons, links, and sections.
- Keep mobile wording aligned with tap-based interactions and desktop wording aligned with click/hover language.

## Layout
- Preserve a responsive, mobile-first layout.
- Keep decorative details subtle so the page still feels clean on small screens.
- Do not place footer-like content at the end of the page unless the task explicitly calls for it.
